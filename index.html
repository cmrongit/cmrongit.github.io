Elephacts!

Natural History

There are only three species of elephants remaining on Earth: the African Savannah Elephant (Loxodonta africana) and the African Forest Elephant (Loxodonta cyclotis) are native to sub-Saharan Africa, and the Asian Elephant (Elephas maximus) can be found in South Asia and Southeast Asia.

Elephants today are descended from a close common ancestor of other giant mammals like Woolly Mammoths and Mastodons, which went extinct only a few thousand years ago.

Charismatic Megafauna

  • Elephants are the largest land mammals on Earth. Due in part to their impressive size, elephants are a keystone species whose survival is crucial to the flourishing of their environments.
  • Adult elephants can grow to between 2.5 - 3.5 meters (8-11 feet) in height and weigh between 1,800 - 6,000 kg.
  • The largest elephant ever recorded was an adult male nicknamed "Henry" who was killed in 1956 and is on exhibit at the Smithsonian Museum of Natural History in Washington, DC. Henry stood 4 meters (13 feet) tall and weighed around 11,000 kg (24,000 lbs).

Elephants & Humans

Elephants are a part of many cultural traditions worldwide – intcluding Ashanti, Hindu, Japanese, and Thai – where they often symbolize strength and wisdon.

Despite this, elephants have also been exploited for profit, conquest, and entertainment. Since before the 6th Century BCE elephants were tamed and trained for use as cavalry mounts in Ancient India; the practice became commonplace in China and Southeast Asia, Persia, and the Mediterranean World. Advances in weapons technologies relegated elephants to support and ceremonial roles by World War I. For centuries, thousands of elephants were killed each year for their tusks–the ivory was used for many things including piano keys, billiard balls, and scrimshaw. Though the international trade in elephant ivory was banned in 1990, poaching and black market trafficking remains a major problem.

Daily Life

  • Elephants are herbivorous: they eat leaves, grasses, roots, twigs, and fruit–up to 150kg of food per day.
  • They sleep around 3-4 hours per night.
  • On average, elephants live about 60-70 years. The oldest elephant on record was named Chengalloor Dakshayani; she died in 2019 at the age of 89.
  • Elephants are highly social animals, not unlike humans. They live in matrilineal family groups, usually of ten or more, including several mothers and their dependent children.
  • Elephants have intelligence, emotional complexity, and self-awareness comparable to that of great apes, corvids, and cetaceans. They have been observed using tools, displaying behaviours associated with grief and altruism, and they have extraordinary memories.

About the Programme

Elephants Are Under Threat

Less than a century ago, there were estimated to be over 3 million elephants on the continent of Africa alone... but in the 21st Century, only around 500,000 elephants remain worldwide.

Around the globe, elephants are under threat from the ivory trade, exploitation for entertainment, and environmental destruction. All three remaining species are listed as endangered by the International Union for the Conservation of Nature (IUCN).

What We Do

Zoos, circuses, and poachers are responsible for breaking up elephant families through death or sale, creating dozens of orphaned elephants every year who require expert care and attention.

The Planetary Pachyderm Placement Progamme was created to help protect these wonderful animals. Our mission is to remove orphaned elephants from circumstances where they are in danger and transport them to natural preserves where they can live out their lives in peace and safety.

How You Can Help

While support in all forms is profoundly appreciated, we need caring people to join our worldwide network and provide sanctuary for these orphans. Unfortunately, our resources are limited and we often need to temporarily place a calf with a foster family until they can be taken to their new homes.

In the wild, elephants live in family groups that provide calves with a caring environment in which to flourish. With your help, the orphans in our care can grow up happy and healthy.
